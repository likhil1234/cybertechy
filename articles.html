Recent Cybercrimes

Digital Arrest Fraud

Digital arrest fraud is a sophisticated scam that has recently emerged in India, where fraudsters impersonate law enforcement to extort money. This type of cybercrime led to significant financial losses of ₹120 crore across 4,599 cases in early 2024. The scams often involve video calls to create urgency and appear legitimate.

Trading and Investment Scams

Trading scams caused ₹1,420 crore in losses, with fraudulent investment schemes luring victims. Additionally, investment scams led to ₹222 crore in losses, exploiting people with promises of high returns but ultimately leading to financial ruin.

Prevention Methods

  • Awareness and Vigilance: Recognize scam tactics and be cautious.
  • Verification: Contact agencies directly if you receive suspicious calls.
  • Protect Personal Information: Do not share sensitive details over unsolicited calls.
  • Stay Calm: Scammers create urgency to manipulate; think critically.
  • Report Suspicious Activity: Use the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal.
  • Preserve Evidence: Keep records of all communications for reporting purposes.

UPI Overpayment Scam

Fraudsters send a small amount and claim it was sent by mistake, asking the victim to return it urgently. This scam leverages empathy, leading victims to transfer larger sums, with losses typically ranging from ₹10,000 to ₹50,000.

QR Code Scams

Fraudsters send QR codes via messaging apps, claiming rewards or discounts. When scanned, funds are debited from the victim’s account instead. Losses generally range from ₹5,000 to ₹30,000.

Prevention Tips

  • Verify Claims: Avoid transferring money without verification.
  • Be Cautious of QR Codes: Only scan codes from trusted contacts or sources.
  • Confirm Intent: Always verify the recipient’s intent before making any transfers.
