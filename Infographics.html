Cybercrime Trends and Resolution in India (2015-2023)

This page provides a detailed overview of cybercrime trends in India from 2015 to 2023, based on data from the National Crime Records Bureau (NCRB). The analysis highlights the drastic rise in cybercrime cases, categorizes the types of cybercrimes reported in 2023, and examines the resolution rate of these cases, emphasizing the urgent need for stronger cybersecurity measures and improved investigative resources.

Increase in Cybercrime Cases (2015-2023)

Year Number of Cases Notes
2015 4,000 Initial recorded data for cybercrime cases.
2017 8,000 Cases doubled within two years.
2019 20,000 Significant increase observed.
2020 49,000 COVID-19 pandemic led to a surge in online activities.
2022 65,983 A 24.4% increase from the previous year.
2023 1,556,218 Drastic rise; over 1.5 million cases reported.

Types of Cybercrimes Committed (NCRB 2023)

Type of Cybercrime Percentage Description
Online Fraud 40% The most prevalent form of cybercrime.
Identity Theft 25% Commonly involves misuse of personal information.
Phishing 15% Attempts to deceive individuals to obtain sensitive data.
Hacking 10% Unauthorized access to information or systems.
Cyberstalking 5% Harassment or stalking of individuals online.
Cyberbullying 3% Intimidating or threatening others through digital means.
Ransomware 2% Malicious software used to hold data hostage.

Resolution Rate of Cybercrime Cases in India

As depicted in the chart, only 25% of cybercrime cases in India are resolved, while 75% remain unsolved or under investigation. This significant gap highlights the challenges faced by law enforcement due to limited resources, the evolving nature of cyber threats, and the complexity of investigating digital crimes. It underscores the need for more resources, improved training, and advanced tools to aid in the timely resolution of cybercrime cases.
