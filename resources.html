Prevention Tips

Protect yourself from cybercrime with these practical steps:

Secure Your Accounts

Use strong passwords and enable 2FA wherever possible.

Avoid Suspicious Links

Don’t click on suspicious links or attachments.

Update Software

Keep your OS, apps, and antivirus software up to date.

Cyber Hygiene Tools

Antivirus Software:

Antivirus software helps detect, block, and remove malware such as viruses, spyware, and ransomware. It is essential for protecting your data and devices.

  • Avast – Free and premium antivirus for home and business use.
  • Norton – Comprehensive protection with identity theft and VPN services.
  • Kaspersky – Known for powerful malware detection and web protection.
Password Managers:

Password managers securely store and generate strong passwords for all your online accounts, reducing the risk of hacks and reuse of weak credentials.

  • LastPass – Cloud-based password manager with autofill and vault features.
  • Bitwarden – Open-source password manager with strong encryption.
  • 1Password – Easy-to-use password vault for individuals and teams.
VPN Services:

Virtual Private Networks (VPNs) encrypt your internet traffic and mask your IP address, enhancing privacy and enabling safe browsing even on public Wi-Fi.

  • NordVPN – Secure, fast, and user-friendly VPN service.
  • ExpressVPN – High-speed VPN with strong privacy protections.
  • ProtonVPN – Swiss-based VPN focused on security and freedom.
Secure Browsers:

Secure browsers provide enhanced privacy settings, block trackers, and protect against phishing. They are essential for safe and anonymous browsing.

  • Brave – Blocks ads and trackers by default, focused on privacy.
  • Mozilla Firefox – Open-source browser with customizable privacy controls.
